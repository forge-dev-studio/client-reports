What this looks at

Before writing a single page, it is worth knowing what people actually search for, how many of them there are, and how hard those searches are to win.

We took every service Legacy Land Care offers and expanded it into the full set of real Google searches around it. For each one we pulled the monthly search volume, both nationwide and inside Georgia, scored how competitive it is to rank for, and checked who currently shows up. The goal was to separate demand that is realistic for a newer website to win from demand that is not, and to find the searches no local competitor has claimed. Two clusters stood out.

The honest read

The very large nationwide numbers, the 49,500 figures, are mostly people researching a topic rather than hiring, and a newer, lower-authority website will not rank for those nationally any time soon. Pretending otherwise would set a false expectation.

The winnable, valuable slices are the Georgia cost terms, the 720 and 210 monthly searches, and the how-to guides written specifically for Georgia red clay. That local framing is what sets the pages apart from generic national content and catches the nearby long-tail searches that turn into calls. The best single opportunity in the whole study is the french drain cost page: money intent, low competition, no local competitor, and confirmed Georgia demand.

A couple of honest judgment calls

Not every search with volume is worth chasing, and saying so is part of the job.

Pond excavation shows real search demand and is plausibly a fit as dirt work. It is worth a quick confirmation of whether Legacy takes on farm and livestock pond work before we target it, rather than assuming.

Deliberately skipped: "drain cleaning," despite huge volume, is indoor plumbing for clogged house drains, a different business entirely. Hydro and vacuum excavation is a specialized service that needs equipment Legacy does not run. Chasing demand that does not match the actual services would waste effort and mislead the people who click.
