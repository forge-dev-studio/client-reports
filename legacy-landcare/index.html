What this measures

When someone searches for a service near them, Google shows a short list of local businesses above the regular results. That short list, the map pack, is where the phone calls come from. Being fourth means being invisible.

Each search combined a service with a city, for example Land Clearing Rome GA or Gravel Driveway Cartersville GA. We recorded Legacy Land Care's position, and when Legacy was not first, which company was. Positions come from Google's local results, measured from each city in turn, not from a single office looking outward.

Where Legacy stands today

The strongest ground is right at home. In Rome, Legacy appears in the local results for 25 of 30 searches and reaches the top 3 on 6 of them, with the best positions on Lot Clearing (#1), Land Clearing Services (#2), Fence Line Clearing (#2). The clearing and mulching searches are further along than the grading and earthwork searches, which is a normal early pattern and a useful signal about where the listing already has traction.

The reputation behind the listing is genuinely strong for a young company: 5 stars across 23 reviews. Customers who find Legacy clearly rate the work. The gap is not the reputation. It is how many of these everyday searches surface the listing in the first place.

Google is not ranking these businesses on reputation. It is ranking them on how clearly it understands what they do and where they do it. That is the good news: a review lead takes years to build, and the visibility layer that sits on top of it is the part that can be worked on directly.

The pattern is distance

Legacy appears in the local results in 9 of 38 markets and reaches the top 3 in 5, with the visibility thinning quickly the further a search sits from Rome. Presence reaches out to about 23.1 miles, and beyond the home cluster the listing largely drops out of the local results. That is expected for a business this new, and it is exactly the map that shows where the next work pays off: the nearby markets where Legacy is close but not yet in the pack.
