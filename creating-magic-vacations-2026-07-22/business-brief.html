Business & group travel

A short read on three lanes that sit beside the vacation market: corporate travel, incentive trips, and group travel. How people find these services, how big the Georgia demand is, and who holds the space today.

These lanes work differently

Worth understanding before reading the numbers.

The vacation market in the main study is a search market: families type "disney travel agent" into Google, and being visible wins the call. Corporate, incentive, and group travel are mostly a relationship market. A company books its trips through the agency its people already know and trust, and it renews there year after year. That difference shapes everything below: the searching is thin, but the work is real and it repeats.

The takeaway. Statewide, these searches run to a few dozen a month each, and most of that sits in metro Atlanta. That is not a knock on the lanes, it is the nature of them: businesses do not shop for a travel manager on Google, they get referred to one. So this is a market you win through relationships and reputation, not through ranking.

Lane by lane

Corporate & business travel

Thin search demand, concentrated in Atlanta, bought by referral. In Rome the space is wide open, but open because few people search it locally rather than because it is untapped gold. This is an outbound and relationship lane: local employers, existing clients, and word of mouth, not search rankings.

Incentive & meeting travel

A specialized corporate lane, held in Atlanta by agencies with dedicated meetings-and-events operations. It rewards that infrastructure, so it is the least natural fit of the three for a personal advisory.

Group travel

The most promising of the three, and the closest to what you already do. You show up in the Rome group results today. The one large local name specializes in faith-based bus tours, which is a single niche, not the whole lane. Family reunions, milestone-celebration groups, girlfriend getaways, and group Disney and cruise trips are the natural openings.

The natural extension

Group travel is not a new direction so much as a bigger version of your current one. A group Disney trip, a family-reunion cruise, or a milestone celebration for a dozen travelers is the same specialty you are already known for, sized up. It comes through the relationships and reviews you are already building, which is exactly why it fits where corporate and incentive do not.
