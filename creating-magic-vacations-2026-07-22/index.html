What this study looks at

Two questions: how big is the market, and where do you show up in it.

The thirty searches here were not picked at random. They are the phrases that real Georgia travel agencies actually get found for, cross checked against Google's own search data, so the list reflects how people in this area look for someone to plan a trip: by type of trip more than by the words "travel agent."

When someone searches something like "disney travel agent Cartersville GA", Google answers first with a short list of nearby businesses beside a little map. This study tracks that local map listing for all thirty searches, in each of twenty towns, out to fifty miles. Alongside it, Google's search-volume data tells us how many people are typing those searches in each town, which is what "the size of the market" means below.

How to read the numbers. Everything was measured on one day in July, and search results move a little day to day, so treat any single number as approximate and the overall shape as the real signal. The market figures are monthly search estimates from Google for your towns, so they describe demand, not a promise.

Where the market is, and where you show up

The one pattern worth sitting with.

Your visibility and the region's demand point in opposite directions. Close to home, inside twenty miles, you reach the top three on about 42 percent of searches. That is the fortress: Cave Spring, Cedartown, Rome, Rockmart, Tallapoosa. But those towns are small, and they hold only a slice of the demand.

The larger towns further out, Kennesaw, Dallas, Douglasville, Dalton, Carrollton, Gadsden, are where 61 percent of the whole market's searching happens. In that outer ring today you reach the top three on about 6 percent of searches. In other words, you are strongest where the fewest people are looking, and least visible where the most people are.

The encouraging part. This is not a wall, it is a frontier. In Cartersville, one of those larger outer towns, you already appear on twenty-three of thirty searches, just below the top three rather than shut out. The same is starting in Gadsden and Calhoun. The pattern that made the home towns work is already reaching outward; it simply has not crossed into the top three in the bigger markets yet.

Disney is your specialty, and the market agrees

Where your deepest strength and the biggest demand overlap.

Disney is the second largest pocket of demand in the region, about 1,140 searches a month, and its two busiest phrases are the most searched terms of any kind locally. It is also where you already have the widest footprint: you appear in up to 16 of the twenty towns for Disney searches, more places than for almost anything else.

So the picture is a specialty you are known for, sitting on top of the demand the market wants most, with your listing already present across the map. That overlap is the clearest single opportunity in this whole study, and it is one you are already halfway into.
