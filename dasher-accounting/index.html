Perspective

What the biggest players north of Atlanta teach us.

We also profiled the five largest-audience bookkeeping and accounting practices in the north Atlanta metro, the businesses a searcher there sees first. Three lessons carry straight back to your market:

1. A big review audience and a visible website are separate wins.

The two practices with the largest review counts up there, at 223 and 178 reviews, have websites that rank for essentially nothing. Reviews carried them into the map results, and their websites carry nothing at all. The winners you should copy hold both.

2. The strongest sites are built around the exact phrases buyers type.

The best-performing site we profiled ranks for the entire "bookkeeping services for small business" family of phrases, thousands of national searches a month, because those words appear throughout its pages in plain, readable text. That phrase architecture is a template, and it is exactly what your rebuilt site will be constructed around.

3. Bookkeeping-first positioning is underused, even in the metro.

Most big-audience practices lead with tax work. A practice that plants its flag on bookkeeping, payroll, and QuickBooks has less company at the top, in the metro and even more so in your 40 miles.
