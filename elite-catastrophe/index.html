What this measures

Positions come from Google Search itself, not from the Maps app, because Search is what most customers actually use.

The storm work already finds Elite

The strongest results in this audit are exactly the jobs Elite is known for. In Rome, Elite sits at #2 for storm damage tree removal, #6 for fallen tree removal and #9 for emergency tree removal, and the storm search carries beyond Floyd County: Elite appears for it in 8 cities, as far out as Calhoun at 21 miles. On top of that, the reputation behind the listing is genuinely elite: 4.9 stars across 76 reviews, and the review count is growing at roughly three times the pace of the two businesses that lead the Rome pack. Customers who find Elite clearly love the work.

The opportunity is that everyday tree searches, the ones people type all year round, don't yet show what storm customers already know.

Reviews are not what's missing

Elite reaches the Rome top 3 on 1 of 30 searches while sitting in the top 10 for 4 of the 15 tree services. The natural assumption is that the businesses ahead simply have more reviews. The searches show otherwise.

Google is not ranking these businesses on reputation. It is ranking them on how clearly it understands what they do and where they do it.

That is good news. A review lead like Elite's takes years to build and cannot be bought. The visibility layer that sits on top of it is the part that can be worked on directly, and the businesses currently ahead prove how quickly Google rewards it.

Roofing is two different stories

On storm roofing searches, Elite already registers: #13 in Rome for both storm damage roof repair and roof tarping, ahead of most roofing companies in town on those terms. The storm-recovery identity is real and Google sees it.

Head-on roofing searches are a different landscape. Rome's roofing pack is held by companies whose entire review base was earned on roofing: 505, 610 and 179 reviews at the top. Those are multi-year positions, and this audit would be doing Elite a disservice to pretend otherwise. The storm side of roofing is where the near-term ground is.
