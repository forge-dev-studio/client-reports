Elite Catastrophe: Local Search Audit & Work Plan

Measured July 21, 2026. 1,140 real Google searches: 30 search terms (15 roofing, 15 tree) across 38 cities within 50 miles of Rome, plus a study of the highest-ranking roofers north of Atlanta and real monthly search volumes.

What this measures

Every number in this report comes from a real, location-targeted Google search run on July 21. For each of 38 cities (every Georgia place of 2,000+ people within 50 miles of Rome, plus Lindale), we searched all 30 terms the way a local customer would, for example tree removal Cartersville GA, and recorded exactly where Elite Catastrophe appears in the local results, along with who holds the top spots.

The map pack (the top 3 businesses Google shows with the map) is where the calls come from. Positions 4 and below are visible only after a click. Not present means the business did not appear in the top 20 local results at all.

Stated plainly: the limits of this data

Local rankings move daily and vary slightly by the searcher's exact location; single positions can drift by one spot. Search volumes are Google Ads annual averages: storm-related terms spike far above their average in the weeks after severe weather, so their yearly numbers understate what they are worth in the moments that matter to this business. Nothing here is a guarantee of future rankings. It is an honest baseline to measure progress against.

The headline

Your reviews are elite. Your visibility is not, and reviews are not the reason.

Across all 1,140 searches, Elite Catastrophe reached the map pack exactly once: #2 in Rome for storm damage tree removal. Yet your Google profile stands at 4.9 stars with 76 reviews, which is more reviews than five of the eight businesses in Rome's tree service pack, and you are adding reviews roughly three times faster than the two businesses at the top.

What is holding you back is how Google has you filed. Your listing and website present a storm damage restoration company first. Google agrees, and ranks you for storm terms. But everyday searches like tree service and tree removal carry most of the demand, and for those Google does not see you as a primary answer yet. That is a fixable configuration problem, not a years-long reputation problem.

What the numbers mean

  • The constraint is identity, not reputation. Google has you filed as storm restoration. The everyday tree terms carry the demand, and your review base already qualifies you for them.
  • Rome first, then the ring. Proximity rules the pack. Rome and the 0 to 25 mile ring (Lindale, Cedartown, Rockmart, Calhoun, Summerville, Adairsville, Cartersville) are winnable; the far metro edge is not, and we will not spend your money pretending it is.
  • Your review engine is the growth asset. You add roughly 11 to 12 reviews a month, about triple the pace of the businesses holding #1 and #2 in Rome. At current paces you pass the #2 business's review count in under a year.
  • Storms are your moat, not your ceiling. Keep owning the storm moment (you are #2 for it in Rome and reach 20+ miles). The fix is adding the everyday tree identity on top, not replacing the storm one.

The plan

  1. Google Business Profile surgery (week 1). Commit the profile to tree service plus storm restoration: category set, full services list, service area matched to the real 0 to 25 mile ring, photo and posting cadence, and a direct review link. Reconcile the hours shown across the web (listings currently disagree about weekend and 24/7 availability).
  2. Citation and trust cleanup (weeks 1 to 2). One exact business name, address, and phone everywhere: today the name appears four different ways across directories, and one legacy lead-gen listing needs to be disassociated or rebuilt. Bring every statement of credentials, memberships, and business history on the site and directories current and verifiable, correct the BBB listing, and display license and insurance details. Clean listings are both a ranking signal and what insurance-minded storm customers check.
  3. Website rebuild (weeks 2 to 6). Tree-first homepage (the current site leads with roofing on a listing that ranks for tree work), a page per service, a page per city in the first ring, and the structured business data (schema) Google reads; the current site has none. Wire the site to the profile, reviews, and socials; today it links to none of them.
  4. Formalize the review engine (ongoing). Your organic pace is already the best in the market. A simple automated ask after every completed job protects the 4.9 and compounds the one advantage the incumbents cannot match.
  5. Storm and insurance content lane (ongoing). The questions homeowners actually search (a tree fell on my roof, who do I call; does insurance cover tree removal; what does emergency tarping cost) have zero good local answers in this market. This is the Dr Roof city grid plus the content engine, aimed at your specialty.

Honest expectations: storm and emergency pack positions in roughly 3 to 6 months, everyday tree term pack entry in Rome in roughly 6 to 12, and a genuine run at #1 and #2 as the review counts converge over 18 to 24. Roofing head terms stay off the promise list; the storm roofing niche is the lane.
