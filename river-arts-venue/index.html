What to do next, in order

  1. Go after the wedding lane. "Wedding venue" is the highest value search on this list and the only one where you sit outside the top 5. The farms own it because their profiles are saturated with wedding photos and wedding reviews. Countermove: add a wedding photo set to the profile, ask every wedding and reception client for a review that uses the word "wedding", and keep the wedding page on the site growing. Your pitch is the one thing farms cannot offer: downtown, indoor, rain proof, with a full kitchen.
  2. Turn events into reviews, by name. You have 6 reviews; Google reads the words inside them. A review that says "birthday party" lifts birthday searches. After each event, send the host one review ask that names their event type. This directly attacks the three amber rows (weddings, birthdays, rehearsal dinners).
  3. Claim the directory shelf space. Page one of the regular website results belongs to PartySlate, Eventective, The Knot, and romegeorgia.org. Create or complete the River Arts listing on each so the pages that outrank you still sell you.
  4. Keep the profile momentum. The 26 services and the first post went live July 19. Hold the cadence: one post and a few fresh photos each month. Google rewards active profiles, and every service you listed matches a search on this scoreboard.
  5. Re-measure in mid August. Same 20 searches, so movement is visible line by line.
