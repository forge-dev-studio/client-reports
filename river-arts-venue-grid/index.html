What this measures

When someone searches for a venue, Google shows a small map with three businesses on it. That block — the "map pack" — is where the clicks and calls go. This report answers one question: for each search, in each town, is River Arts in that block?

Each search was localised to the town it names, so the Cartersville column reflects what a person standing in Cartersville sees, not a national average. Alongside each term is its real monthly search volume, so the ranking can be weighed against how much demand it represents.

Rome belongs to River Arts

Eighteen of twenty searches put River Arts in the map pack at home, and fifteen put it at #1 — ahead of every other venue in town. That is as strong as a home market gets, and it was built in under a year with six reviews.

Only two Rome searches fall outside the top three. Both are wedding searches: wedding venue (#5) and rehearsal dinner venue (#4). That is not a coincidence — it is the pattern that runs through this entire report.

The wall is at five miles, not thirty

Lindale sits 5.1 miles away. River Arts still reaches the pack for half its searches there — but ranks #1 for none of them, and disappears entirely from five, including every wedding term. The venue taking those: The Mill in Lindale, with 130 reviews.

Past that, the drop is total. Across Adairsville, Euharlee, Cedartown, Summerville, Rockmart, Calhoun, Cartersville and Dallas — 160 searches — River Arts does not appear once. Not at #4, not at #20. Those towns hold roughly 100,000 people between them and they cannot find it.

Distance is not the ceiling

It would be easy to read that wall as geography — people book venues close to home, so of course a Rome venue does not rank in Dallas. The data says otherwise.

Magnolia Creek Farms holds a top-three spot in five different towns

Lindale, Adairsville, Euharlee, Calhoun and Cartersville — a spread of roughly 5 to 22 miles. Spring Lake Events covers three towns. Hightower Falls covers three. River Arts covers two: Rome and, partially, Lindale.

Venues in this market do rank across a wide area. The ones that manage it are the ones Google understands as regional wedding destinations, and they carry 70 to 130 reviews saying so. River Arts is read as a Rome business, full stop.

One more thing worth knowing: of the 94 venues that hold a top-three spot somewhere in this grid, 15 have fewer reviews than River Arts does. Review count alone is not the barrier — relevance for the search is.

Demand sits exactly where you are weakest

Wedding searches are not one category among twenty. In Rome they are the market. "Wedding venues near me" is searched about 90 times a month and "wedding venue" about 70 — while nearly every other venue term sits at 10.

So the searches River Arts wins are real, but thin. The one search that carries the most demand in town is the one where it sits at #10.

What we would do next

  1. Win the wedding search in Rome first Before chasing other towns, take the two home searches still outside the pack. That means wedding photography on the profile, the wedding and reception services listed (done 19 July), and — most of all — reviews from wedding clients that say the word "wedding". Google reads review text.
  2. Ask every single client for a review Six reviews is a remarkable foundation for fifteen #1 rankings, and it is also the ceiling. The venues holding multiple towns carry 70 to 130. Every event that leaves the building should end with one request, named for the occasion it was: "wedding", "baby shower", "corporate training".
  3. Give Google a reason to show you outside Rome The eight silent towns are not a distance problem — rivals span them. What they lack is any signal that River Arts serves them. Town-specific pages on the site, the service area set properly on the profile, and photos or posts that name real events from Cartersville, Cedartown and Calhoun clients.
  4. Take the Lindale near-miss Ten pack placements at five miles with zero #1s is the cheapest ground on the board. It is one strong competitor, not a crowd.
  5. Re-measure in mid-August Same 200 searches, same method. The wedding rows and the eight silent towns are the two numbers to watch.
