What this means

Where the eight appearances are

Of 320 searches across 16 towns and 20 terms, the office appears in 8. Six of those are in Rome itself, at positions 15, 17, 17, 18, 18 and 20. The other two are "houses for sale" in Lindale at 11th and "land for sale" in Aragon at 6th, which is the single strongest position anywhere in the study.

Worth saying clearly: in all 320 searches Google did assemble a local pack. There is no market here where Google declines to show local businesses, so every one of these is a contest that is actually being held.

Your reviews are not what is holding you back

This is the most useful thing in the report, because it rules out the lever most people reach for first.

You are at 5.0 stars on 7 reviews. Across the study, 444 distinct businesses hold a top-five spot and review counts were captured for 423 of them. The median is 18. But 143 of those rank with fewer than 10 reviews, and 98 rank with fewer than your 7.

Narrow it to the businesses actually holding top-three spots, which is the block customers see, and the picture is the same. Of the 287 with captured counts, the median is 22, 88 hold their spot with fewer than 10 reviews, and 57 hold a top-three spot with fewer reviews than you have. Belle Jennings Templeton takes seven first-place spots on 2 reviews. Garrett Land Company holds 26 top-five spots across 9 towns, 16 of them top-three, on 4 reviews. Cynthia Weiser at Calhoun GA Homes holds 12 top-three spots on 6.

Your profile shape is also a common one among businesses that do rank: 103 of the holders here are rated 5.0 with fewer than 10 reviews. So 7 reviews already sits inside the range that wins these searches. Climbing toward the 18 to 22 median is worth doing steadily, and it will help at the margin, but the data says something other than review volume is deciding these results. For context on the top end, Toles, Temple & Wright sits at 342 reviews and 4.9 stars and takes 23 first-place spots across 9 towns. That is the ceiling in this market, not the entry price.

Distance is not what is keeping you out either

Before assuming the outlying towns are simply too far, it is worth checking whether the businesses winning them are any closer, and they frequently are not.

There are 86 searches where a business holding the top three sits farther from that town than your office does, allowing a five mile margin so near-ties do not count. They span 15 of the 16 towns, and 7 of them are in Rome itself. The starkest is "sell my house" in Rome, where Cash Out House holds first place from 67.2 miles out against your 0.5 miles.

Set aside the terms that belong to other industries, covered below, and 49 of these remain on the core brokerage terms, across 12 towns and including 4 in Rome. Holistic Home Collective accounts for 10, taking top-three spots in Summerville and Trion from over 40 miles out. Relocation Realty takes 7, including first place for "relocation real estate agent" in Summerville from 65 miles and in Trion from 65.4 miles. Atlanta Luxury Homes Group takes second for "luxury homes for sale" in Lindale from 44.8 miles against your 4.5.

Since businesses 40 to 65 miles away can hold these towns, the deciding factor is not the drive. It is what each listing tells Google about where it works and what it does. 242 N 5th Ave is a strong address for this grid, closer to more of these towns than most of the businesses currently ranking in them.

Five of the twenty terms belong to other industries

A quarter of this study, 80 of the 320 searches, is being decided in packs that a sales brokerage is not really eligible for. That is a structural fact about those terms, not a gap on your side, and it changes what the realistic denominator is.

  • Home appraisal: 96 percent of top-three spots go to licensed appraisal firms such as Mac Valuations and Nelson Appraisal Services. Lending rules generally keep agents out of appraising, so this pack was never available.
  • Sell my house and cash home buyers: 95 and 93 percent go to investor and cash-buyer companies. These are also the least local searches in the study, with a median top-three distance of 57.6 and 66.2 miles.
  • New construction homes: 69 percent go to builders and named subdivisions, D.R. Horton and Ryan Homes among them.
  • Property management: 65 percent go to dedicated rental managers, which is a different license focus.

These five also account for 37 of the 86 searches where someone farther away is winning, which is what you would expect when the competition is a regional cash buyer rather than a local brokerage. Set them aside and the real target is the other 15 terms, 240 searches, and those behave completely differently.

Rome is a local contest, and it is winnable

The core terms are decided among genuinely local businesses. For "real estate agent" the median top-three holder is 3 miles from the centre of town, and for "realtor" it is 5 miles. Compare that with the 57 and 66 mile medians on the investor terms and you can see these are two different kinds of search.

The five businesses holding the core Rome terms are all within 2.3 miles of you: Toles, Temple & Wright at 342 reviews, Elite Group Georgia at 109, Sign Your Deed Realty at 65, All Star Property Group at 57, and Keller Williams Realty Northwest at 53. Four of those five are under 110 reviews. You are already appearing on six of these Rome searches, which means Google has the listing associated with the right town, and your category is correctly set to Real estate agency. The gap is prominence, not eligibility.

Three things that would move the needle

Take the towns where you already have the distance advantage

This is the biggest lever in the report and it is a settings review rather than a project. There are 86 searches where a business holding the top three is farther from that town than you are, spread across 15 of the 16 towns and including 7 in Rome itself. On the core brokerage terms alone that is 49 searches across 12 towns. Holistic Home Collective holds Summerville and Trion from over 40 miles out. Relocation Realty takes first place in both from 65 miles.

When businesses that far away are winning towns you are closer to, the deciding factor is what each listing tells Google about the areas it works in and the services it offers, rather than where the office actually sits. That makes the coverage and service settings on your Business Profile the thing worth going through next. Cedartown, Lindale, Shannon, Cave Spring and Trion are where this shows up most, and a downtown Rome office should be competitive in all of them on proximity alone. You can do this yourself this week without involving anyone.

Build on the seven reviews you already have

Your 5.0 rating is as strong as anything in this market, and at 7 reviews you are already inside the range that ranks: 98 of the 423 businesses holding spots here have fewer, and 57 businesses hold a top-three spot with fewer than you.

So this is a short climb rather than a standing start, and it is worth being clear that it is a supporting move rather than the fix. The median business holding a top-three spot has 22 reviews, which puts about fifteen between you and the middle of the field. Recent closings are the natural place to draw from, and a brokerage operating at 242 N 5th Ave has a back catalogue to work through. Making the ask a routine part of closing is the version of this that compounds, and it runs quietly in the background while the other two items get sorted.

Give the Rome office its own identity on the brokerage site

The brokerage runs on the shared Realty ONE Group Edge corporate platform, and the page title currently reads "Realty ONE Group Edge | WOODSTOCK Real Estate". Woodstock is roughly 50 miles from Rome. Google leans heavily on the website connected to a listing to work out which town a business belongs to, so a Rome office presented under another town's name is working against the listing rather than supporting it.

What would help is a page that is unambiguously about this office: Rome in the title, the 242 N 5th Ave address, the office's own agents, and the counties you actually cover across Floyd, Polk, Bartow, Gordon and Chattooga. It is the longest of the three to arrange, which is why it sits last, and it is the one item here that needs a decision above the office level. As the owner's daughter you are better placed than most agents to get that changed at the brokerage level rather than working around it.
