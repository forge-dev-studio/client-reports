The short version

The searches an advertiser would run are live and healthy, and magazines like yours are winning them. What Google does not yet connect to the Rome area is your listing. Across 320 searches in 16 towns, Google returned a real list of local businesses 318 times. Only two came back empty. Your listing itself is in good order, rated 4.5 on 15 reviews, and it is registered at 1420 Fritz St SE in Cleveland, Tennessee, which sits between 43.7 and 83.7 miles from the towns measured here, with no second listing anywhere in the Rome area. That one fact explains far more of this report than anything about wording or category does, and the encouraging part is that the businesses beating you to these spots are mostly reaching in from much farther away than you are.

What this means

The packs are there, and magazines are in them

This audit was set up expecting a different result. The working assumption was that a magazine publisher would rarely surface at all on advertising searches, because Google would either show no local list or fill it with digital ad agencies. The data does not support that.

Of the 320 searches, 318 returned a local list of businesses. The two exceptions were both "advertising sales rep", in Lindale and in Aragon, where Google returned nothing local at all. That is a property of those two searches, not a reflection on any business.

More to the point, community magazines are holding these spots right now. Community Magazine appears in the top five in 14 of the 16 towns on "community magazine advertising" and takes first place in eight of them. Highland Living Magazine holds 17 top-five spots across eight towns. IN Community Magazines holds 13 across nine towns. Buy Local Magazine takes first place in Lindale, Euharlee and Aragon. Acworth Community Magazine holds spots in seven towns. The category is not closed to a publisher. It is being actively won by publishers.

Distance is not what is deciding these searches

This is the finding that changes what to do next, so it is worth walking through the numbers.

Your listing sits 62.5 miles from Rome, 43.7 from Calhoun, 47.4 from Trion, 52.3 from Summerville and 83.7 from Dallas at the far end. Those are real distances. But look at who is ranking in the same towns:

  • Community Magazine holds a top-five spot in 14 of these 16 towns from between 733.6 and 781.1 miles away, and is first in eight of them.
  • Highland Living Magazine is farther from the town than you are in six of the eight towns it ranks in: 106.4 miles from Trion against your 47.4, 91.2 from Shannon against your 55.7, 81.4 from Lindale against your 66.8, 77.9 from Euharlee against your 68, 85 from Centre against your 81.7 and 77.2 from Cave Spring against your 75.2. It takes first place in both Cave Spring and Centre.
  • Buy Local Magazine takes first place in Lindale, Euharlee and Aragon from between 253.4 and 276.4 miles.

In 20 separate captured searches a rival holds a position while sitting farther from that town than you are. Trion is the clearest example: rivals hold spots there from 74, 63.2, 60.4, 59.9 and 56.7 miles while you are 47.4 miles out.

When results look like that, the deciding factor is not how close a business is. It is what the business has told Google about where it works. That is a settings question, and settings are free to change.

Reviews are not the lever here

Your 4.5 rating on 15 reviews is a genuine asset, and the data says it is not what is holding you back.

Across all 320 searches, 905 top-three positions were captured. 423 of them are held by businesses with fewer than 15 reviews. Community Magazine takes first place eight times on a 4.8 rating from 8 reviews, which is fewer than you have. In Rome, the top spot for "advertising agency", "advertising company" and "advertise my business" all belong to Advertising Dynamics Inc on a 3.7 rating from 3 reviews.

Reputation is already an area where you compare well. Something else is deciding these lists.

Four searches are where a publisher genuinely competes

Splitting the 20 search terms by whether any magazine or publisher shows up in the results at all makes the map very clear.

  • community magazine advertising: a magazine or publisher appears in 15 of the 16 town results
  • local magazine advertising: 14 of 16
  • magazine advertising: 11 of 16
  • magazine ad rates: 8 of 16

Now the other end. "Church marketing services", "small business marketing" and "direct mail advertising" returned no magazine or publisher in any of their 48 combined results. "Marketing agency", "advertising agency" and "advertise my business" each returned exactly one across 16 towns. Those lists belong to digital agencies and sign shops: Strategic Visibility, Denim Marketing, Triple Take Marketing, Here Local Media and similar.

So the original concern about these terms was half right. The broad marketing phrases really are another industry's ground and are not worth chasing. The four magazine phrases are a different story, and those are the ones your advertisers would actually type.

The faith angle is wide open locally

Worth flagging because it is a real differentiator that nobody nearby has claimed.

In Rome, "christian advertising" and "faith based marketing" are both led by Christian Taylor Marketing, captured at 2,465.7 miles from Rome. "Christian business directory" in Rome is led by ChristianYellowPages at 2,006.4 miles. Across the whole grid, Faithworks Marketing holds 29 top-three positions in 13 of the 16 towns on a 5.0 rating from 6 reviews, and Christian Taylor Marketing holds first place 19 times across 14 towns.

Every one of those is a national operation with no presence in northwest Georgia. There is no local faith-based advertising business holding this ground, which is unusual and will not stay true forever.

What the listing currently says

Your Google listing is categorised as Publisher. That is an accurate description of what the business produces. The 320 searches measured here are not run by readers though, they are run by business owners looking for somewhere to place an ad, and the results Google assembles for them are lists of places that sell advertising. A listing described by its product rather than by what it sells to a local business is a plausible part of why it does not appear on those lists, and it is one of the few things on this page that can be adjusted in an afternoon.

Three things that would move the needle

Put the Rome edition on Google's map of the Rome area

This is the highest-leverage item in the report by a wide margin. Your listing is registered in Cleveland, which is correct for where the business is based, and right now it gives Google nothing that ties the magazine to Floyd, Polk, Bartow, Chattooga or Gordon county. Google is left to work out your relevance to Rome from proximity alone, and at 62.5 miles that is a hard case to make on its own.

The businesses appearing instead are not doing anything you cannot do. Community Magazine is ranking in 14 of these 16 towns from more than 730 miles away. Highland Living Magazine is ranking in six towns while sitting farther from each of them than you are. Buy Local Magazine is taking first place in three towns from over 250 miles. None of that is proximity. All of it is coverage that has been declared and recorded.

Declaring the towns and counties the Rome edition actually circulates in costs nothing, takes one sitting, and is the change most likely to move this entire page.

Describe the listing by what an advertiser is shopping for

The listing is currently filed under Publisher, which describes the magazine itself. Every search in this audit was chosen to match what a local business owner types when they are looking for somewhere to advertise, and the lists Google builds for those searches are lists of companies that sell ad space.

Bringing the listing's category and description in line with the advertising side of the business, while keeping the publishing identity intact, closes that gap. The same applies to how the services are described: "magazine ad rates", "print advertising" and "community magazine advertising" are phrases advertisers use and they currently have nothing on the listing to attach themselves to.

This is free, it is entirely within your control, and combined with the coverage item above it addresses both halves of how Google decides who belongs on these lists.

Concentrate on the four searches where magazines actually win

The data draws an unusually sharp line here, and following it will save a great deal of effort.

On "community magazine advertising" a magazine or publisher appears in 15 of 16 town results. On "local magazine advertising" it is 14 of 16, on "magazine advertising" 11 of 16, on "magazine ad rates" 8 of 16. These are open, active lists with room in them, and the businesses currently holding those spots have between 1 and 21 reviews.

Against that, "church marketing services", "small business marketing" and "direct mail advertising" produced no magazine or publisher anywhere in 48 combined results, and the broad agency terms produced one each across all 16 towns. Those lists belong to digital marketing firms and are not worth the energy.

Pointing your listing description, your services and the language on your sales materials at those four magazine phrases aims your effort at the ground where publishers are demonstrably winning. Add the faith-based angle to that and you are competing for territory currently held only by companies two thousand miles away.
