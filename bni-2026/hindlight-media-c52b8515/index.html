The short version

Google already shows HindLight Media somewhere in 104 of the 320 searches we ran, across 13 of the 16 towns. The gap is not visibility, it is the top three. What this means

Rome is nearly finished, and that is the best news here

You appear in 18 of the 20 Rome searches. Three are already in the top three: videographer at third, marketing video production at second, product video production at third. Twelve more sit at fourth, fifth or sixth. That means 15 of 20 Rome searches already place you in the top six, and six of those twelve are sitting at fourth, a single row outside the block people actually click.

The twelve are video production, video production company, video production services, local video production, commercial video production, business video production, promotional video production, brand video production, social media video production, explainer video production, church video production and event video production.

Reviews and category are not what is deciding this

You are at 5.0 stars on 14 reviews. In Rome, the two businesses holding a top-three spot in 16 of the 20 searches are Brand RED Studios at 4.7 on 7 reviews and Deno Brown Films at 5.0 on 5 reviews. You have at least twice the reviews of either and a higher rating than one of them.

Further out the same holds. Verified Videos LLC, at 5.0 on 7 reviews, holds a top-three spot in 30 searches across 13 towns and reaches as far as 65 miles to do it, including first place in Rome for promotional video production from 49 miles away. Your profile is also already categorised as a Video production service, so this is not a case of being filed under the wrong heading. Something other than reputation and category is ordering these results.

Your top three ends at twelve miles. Theirs does not

All eight of your top-three placements are in Rome, Lindale and Shannon, none farther than 12 miles from the studio. Compare that with the companies filling the rest of the map:

  • Fable Creature Video Production, 5.0 on 22 reviews, holds the top three in 85 searches across 13 towns, reaching up to 44 miles.
  • Killshot Media, 5.0 on 45, holds 62, winning towns from 40 to 78 miles away.
  • RanDesign Media Services, 5.0 on 25, holds 46, from 49 to 81 miles out.
  • Kelly Rose Productions, 5.0 on 68, holds 40 across 13 towns.

Ten of the 417 are in Rome itself. Verified Videos LLC takes the Rome promotional video pack from 49 miles, Drift Digital Media takes Rome social media video from 29.5, Fable Creature takes Rome event video from 24. When a rival wins a town from farther away than you sit, that pack is being decided by declared coverage rather than by who is closest, and that is a contest you can enter.

Cartersville is the clearest case. You appear in none of its 20 searches, yet fourteen of the companies holding top-three spots there are farther from Cartersville than your studio is, including Killshot at 40 miles, The Pixel Pusher Creative Services at 40, Bolt Entertainment at 37 and BamCam Visuals at 36. The pattern runs hardest in Adairsville, with 44 of these instances, Cedartown with 43 and Cartersville with 33.

Rome is the only town where being local settles it

We checked where the top-three holders in each town are actually based. In Rome, 41 of the 60 top-three slots go to businesses within 5 miles. Every other town in the grid flips: in Lindale 87 percent of top-three slots are held from more than 20 miles away, in Adairsville 86 percent, in Cedartown and Calhoun 83 percent. Region-wide, 22 percent of all top-three slots go to businesses far enough out to be flagged as out of area.

That is what a service-area trade looks like on a map. Outside your own city limits, nobody is winning these packs because of a front door, which means declared coverage carries more weight here than it would for a walk-in business.

Two terms behave differently from the rest

"Videographer" was included knowing it might not behave like the others, and it did not. In Rome, where you rank third, the pack is video companies. Everywhere else it fills with wedding specialists: Zachary Will Weddings Videography, I Do Videography Atlanta, Felix Wedding Films, Karstan Wedding Videography, Film & Fable Wedding Photo and Film. That is a different and much larger trade, those cells are not a fair measure of your visibility, and there is no need to chase them.

"Testimonial video production" is the opposite situation and the most striking single cell in the report. It is the only one of the 20 terms where you appear in none of the 16 towns. In Rome, its top three are held by The Video Plug from 605 miles away, TC Productions from 50 miles and Kelly Rose Productions from 54. Not one Rome business appears in the top three of a Rome search. That is open ground.

Two towns worth knowing about before spending effort there

Centre and Dallas return nothing for you in all 20 searches each, and both are unusual markets. In Centre, the top three is held almost entirely by Alabama companies reaching in from 64 to 127 miles, with Tone Production's Auburn listing alone taking 16 of the 60 available slots. In Dallas, Fable Creature holds a top-three spot in 19 of the 20 searches from 8.8 miles away, and several remaining slots go to companies in Dallas, Texas, roughly 692 miles from Georgia. Those two are the weakest use of your effort on this map.

Worth noting alongside that: all 320 searches returned a local pack. There is no town or term here where Google declines to show local results, so every one of these cells is winnable in principle.

Three things that would move the needle

Tell Google how far the camera travels

This is the widest-reaching item in the report and it is a profile change, not a project. Across this grid there are 417 instances where a video company farther from the town than you are holds a pack spot, and 152 of them are first place. Verified Videos LLC does it in Rome itself, from 49 miles, on 7 reviews. Fable Creature does it in 13 towns. None of those companies has better proximity, a better rating or more reviews than you.

Your top three currently ends at 12 miles, which is the shape of a business Google reads as serving Rome and its immediate suburbs. A production company that regularly drives to Cartersville, Calhoun, Cedartown and Summerville should look like one on the profile. This is something you can do yourself this week, and it is the change with the most cells behind it.

Claim the corporate and testimonial searches no Rome company holds

Two of the highest-value terms in your set are unclaimed by anyone local. Testimonial video production is the only term where you appear in none of the 16 towns, and its Rome pack is led from 605 miles away with second and third held from 50 and 54 miles out. Corporate video production, the term where more rivals rank from farther away than any other in the set at 31 instances, currently places you seventh in Rome and shows you in only one other town in the grid.

These are the searches a business owner runs when they already have a budget and a brief, and they describe work HindLight does. Making those specific service names explicit in how the profile and the site describe the business is a small change aimed at terms where every current answer is an hour's drive from the person searching.

Close the twelve Rome searches sitting at fourth to sixth

This is the most concrete opportunity on the board. In Rome you already appear in 18 of 20 searches and hold the top three in 3. Twelve more sit at fourth, fifth or sixth, and six of those twelve are at fourth. Move them and Rome goes from 3 of 20 to 15 of 20 in the block that customers see.

The same pattern sits next door. Lindale has five more at fourth to sixth and Shannon has six, so 23 of your 27 near misses across the entire 16-town region are inside a 12-mile circle around the studio, in the three towns where you already rank best. Closing a one or two position gap where Google is already showing you is a much shorter distance than winning a town you do not appear in yet, and these three towns are where your actual client base lives.
