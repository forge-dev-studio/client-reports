How this was measured

Every number here comes from Google's local search results, the list a customer actually sees when they search from a given town. We ran 320 individual searches across 20 terms and 16 markets within about 30 miles, measured against your listing at 314 E 1st Ave Ste 701, Rome, GA 30161.

Positions 1 to 3 are the map pack, the three businesses Google shows before a searcher has to click anything. That is where the phone calls come from. Positions 4 and beyond are real but far less valuable, and they tell us how close you are to breaking in.

This deliberately uses Google's search results rather than the Maps app. Maps ranks heavily by how close you are to the person searching, which flatters a business in its own town and hides the true picture everywhere else. Rankings also drift by a position or so day to day, so treat any single number as a reading, not a verdict.

What this means

Your home markets are strong, and one of them is a surprise

Rome puts you in the top three on 13 of 20 searches and you appear in all 20. Lindale is 10 of 20. Your single best market anywhere is Shannon at 15 of 20, a community of about 1,900 people that most practices would never think to check.

Cartersville deserves its own line. You appear in 19 of 20 searches there and hold the top three in six, which beats several towns physically closer to your Rome clinic. That is your second office doing exactly what a second office should do, and the numbers say it was a good decision.

Reviews are not what is holding you back

You are at 5.0 stars on 351 reviews. Every single business that outranks you on the broad terms has fewer: Advance Rehabilitation at 219, the Atrium Health Floyd locations between 28 and 150, Performance Physical Therapy at 12, Rehab Partners at 17.

This matters because "get more reviews" is the standard advice, and in your case the data says it is the wrong lever. You already won that contest. Something else is deciding these searches.

Your weakest terms are the broadest ones

Three searches return no top-three placement anywhere across all 16 markets: "physical therapy clinic", "outpatient physical therapy" and "rehabilitation clinic". Meanwhile "dry needling" puts you in the top three in seven towns, "fall prevention therapy" in six, "manual therapy" and "physical therapist" in five.

Worth noting that your Business Profile is already categorised as a Physical therapy clinic, so this is not a case of being filed under the wrong heading. The broad phrases are simply where the hospital systems and multi-site chains concentrate, and where a single practice has the least room. Your specialty terms are open ground by comparison.

One term, "pain management clinic", was included on purpose as a control. It returns one appearance and no top-three spots, which is the correct result: those searches belong to interventional pain physicians who do injections and spinal procedures. It is not a gap, and it is not worth pursuing.

The outlying towns split into two very different groups

Results thin out past about 14 miles, but the reason differs town by town, and that difference decides whether a town is worth pursuing.

In Cedartown, Rockmart, Calhoun, Cartersville, Adairsville, Summerville and Dallas, more than 90 percent of the businesses holding the top three are based inside that town. PT Solutions has a clinic in Adairsville and another in Calhoun. Atrium Health Floyd has Cedartown and Rockmart locations. These are competitors with a front door on the same street as the patient, in a service people attend twice a week. No profile setting out-ranks a building.

In Aragon, Cave Spring, Cedar Bluff and Centre, the picture reverses. In Aragon, 62 percent of the businesses holding those spots are reaching in from 15 to 45 miles away, and several are mobile or in-home providers such as Restore Mobile PT and MovementX. Cave Spring is 43 percent, the two Alabama towns 36 percent each. Nobody has a building there either, which means those spots are being won on declared coverage rather than on proximity, and that is a contest you can enter.

Separately, in 21 of the 320 searches Google could not assemble a local pack at all and filled it with businesses well outside the region. Those are not losses. They are searches with no established local answer yet.

Three things that would move the needle

Lead with the services that already travel

Dry needling reaches the top three in seven towns. Fall prevention reaches six. Manual therapy and shoulder work reach five and four. The plain phrase "physical therapy" manages two. Most practices lead with the category and tuck the specialties onto a sub-page, and your numbers say that is backwards. Making these the front door of how the practice presents itself aligns your visibility with what is already working. Fall prevention is worth particular attention, since it reaches six towns and speaks to an older patient who searches on their own before asking anyone for a referral.

Claim the four towns that are actually open

Aragon, Cave Spring, Cedar Bluff and Centre are being served largely by providers reaching in from 15 to 45 miles away, several of them mobile therapists with no local premises. That is a very different situation from Cedartown or Calhoun, and it means those top-three spots are being decided by which businesses declare they cover the area rather than by who is nearest. You are closer to all four than most of the providers currently ranking there. This is the cheapest opportunity in the report and it does not require opening anything.

Stop spending on the towns that are locked, and stop buying reviews you do not need

Cedartown, Rockmart, Calhoun, Adairsville, Summerville and Dallas are each held by clinics physically located in the town, and your own Rockmart and Cartersville numbers prove the pattern: the farther town where you have an office massively outperforms the nearer one where you do not. Chasing those through profile work will not move them, and your review count is already ahead of every competitor beating you. If those towns matter commercially, the route is referral relationships with the physicians and employers who send patients, or eventually a location. Cedartown is the one to watch, since you already appear in 11 searches there with no building at all.
