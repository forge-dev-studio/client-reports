The short version

You do not appear in any of the 320 searches measured, and the reason is the most fixable one in this entire chapter audit: there is no profile for Google to show. Here is what makes that worth reading rather than wincing at. On the four searches people type when they already know what they want, 32 individual agents appear by name across these 16 towns, and 300 of the 302 top-five spots those searches hand out belong to a listing showing no reviews at all. Nobody won this ground on reputation or on tenure. They won it by having a profile that exists. That is a door standing open, and it costs nothing to walk through.

What this means

What was measured

Twenty insurance search terms across 16 towns, Rome outward through Floyd, Bartow, Polk, Gordon and Chattooga counties plus Cedar Bluff and Centre in Alabama. That is 320 individual searches. Nothing under your name appears in any of them, which is exactly what the data should show: a local pack can only display profiles that exist, and there is none under your name in the captured results. This is a starting line, not a score.

One note before anything else. In one search of the 320, hospital indemnity insurance in Trion, Google returned no local results whatsoever. That is a property of that search in that town and has nothing to do with any business.

Thirty-two other agents do show up, and that is the whole report

Four of the twenty terms are the ones a buyer types when they already know the product: aflac agent, aflac insurance agent, aflac representative, and independent aflac agent. Four terms across 16 towns is 64 searches, and those 64 searches handed out 302 top-five placements in total.

259 of those 302 placements, 86 percent, went to an Aflac-connected listing. 175 of them went to an individually named agent rather than a corporate page. Thirty-two different people are named.

The ones carrying the most ground:

  • Douglas Eubanks appears in 33 searches across 11 of the 16 towns and holds first place in 19 of them. He is first in Rome for both aflac agent and aflac insurance agent, and he sweeps all four terms in Cartersville and in Euharlee.
  • Phil Littrell appears in 20 searches across 8 towns with 9 first-place finishes, including all four terms in Dallas.
  • Mackenzie Bolden appears in 23 searches across 10 towns.
  • Rick G Cantrell appears in 14 searches across 5 towns, and is the only agent anywhere in the study to reach a non-brand term.
  • Judy M Bird appears in 12 searches across 6 towns, including fifth place in Rome for independent aflac agent.
  • Joyce Gooch holds first place on all four terms in Lindale.

In 14 of the 16 towns, one of these individuals sits at number one on at least one of the four searches. These are not faceless national competitors. They are people doing the same work you do, and the only thing separating their result from yours is a profile.

Nobody won these searches on reviews

This is the number worth sitting with. Of the 302 placements those four searches handed out, 300 show no review count at all. The single highest review total on any listing holding one of those spots anywhere in the 16 towns is four.

Now compare that to the general insurance terms in Rome, where the top spots go to Talley Insurance Services at 134 reviews, Acceptance Insurance at 314, Hitchcock Insurance Group at 402, and Matt Medley State Farm at 603. Those are long contests against firms with years of accumulated standing.

The four brand terms are not that. On those, the entry price is presence, not reputation. Being new to a listing costs you almost nothing here, which is a genuinely unusual position and it will not stay open forever.

Distance is not the gate either

It would be reasonable to assume the agents ranking in Cedartown or Calhoun are simply closer to those towns. The data says otherwise.

Douglas Eubanks holds first place in Rome from 18.8 miles away. Mackenzie Bolden holds top-five placements in Cedartown, Rockmart, Dallas, Cave Spring and six other towns from 128 to 177 miles away. The single Aflac listing that holds the entire Rome result for aflac representative is measured 124 miles out.

Proximity is not deciding these searches. Declared coverage is. You are physically nearer to Rome than the agent currently ranking first in it, and nearer to most of these 16 towns than the agents currently holding them.

Where the field is thinnest right now

Some of these searches are barely contested at all, and a new profile surfaces fastest where there is the least to move past.

  • Rome, aflac representative: the entire local result set is one listing, the corporate Aflac page 124 miles away. This is the thinnest search in the whole study, and it is in your home town.
  • Cartersville: aflac agent returns 1 result, aflac insurance agent and aflac representative return 2 each. One agent holds all of them.
  • Dallas: between 3 and 5 results on all four terms.
  • Independent aflac agent is the thinnest term overall: 4 results in Shannon, 5 in Adairsville, Summerville and Dallas, 6 in Euharlee, and 12 in Rome, against a normal full pack of 20.

The general terms are a different, longer conversation

The other 16 terms behave nothing like the brand ones. Supplemental insurance, disability insurance, accident insurance, hospital indemnity insurance, group insurance broker and life insurance agent in Rome are all held by established multi-line agencies: Talley Insurance Services, Acceptance Insurance, Coosa Valley Financial Group, Rome Healthplan Advisors, Velox Insurance. Those are worth revisiting once a profile is established and has some history behind it, not on day one.

Three of the twenty are structural rather than competitive, and they are worth knowing about so they do not read as gaps:

  • Cancer insurance in Rome returns Cancer Navigators, two Harbin Clinic oncology locations, AdventHealth Redmond and Atrium Health Floyd. That search belongs to cancer treatment, not cancer coverage. Interestingly, Rick G Cantrell does hold first place on it in Dallas from 2.5 miles with a 5.0 rating on 4 reviews, the one time an agent breaks into that term anywhere in the study.
  • Voluntary benefits in Rome returns the Labor Department, the Social Security Administration and a company measured 2,465 miles away. Google is not treating it as a local business search.
  • Long term disability insurance returns general agencies in all 16 towns with no Aflac-connected listing anywhere. That fits the product line: long-term disability is typically an employer-group product placed through other carriers, so this is a category boundary rather than a missed opportunity.

Three things that would move the needle

Claim a Google Business Profile in your own name

This is the highest-return move available to anyone in this audit, and it costs nothing but an afternoon. Every one of the 32 agents appearing across these 16 towns has a profile. That is the only thing they have that you do not, and it is the reason 320 searches came back empty.

The most striking single number in the report makes the case on its own: in Rome, the search aflac representative returns exactly one local result, a corporate page measured 124 miles away. There is currently no local human answer to that search in your own town.

One practical note worth a phone call before you publish: how you title the listing is worth a quick check against your carrier's marketing and trademark guidelines. Agents in the data use several different naming patterns, and your compliance contact will tell you which one is approved for your contract.

Name every town you actually serve

Once the profile exists, the service area you declare determines how far it reaches, and the data is unusually clear on this point. Mackenzie Bolden holds top-five spots in Cedartown, Rockmart, Dallas and seven other towns from between 128 and 177 miles away. The agent sitting first in Rome is 18.8 miles out. None of that is proximity doing the work, it is declared coverage.

You are closer to Rome than the agent currently ranking there, and closer to most of these 16 towns than the agents holding them. Listing the towns you genuinely cover is what turns one profile into presence across a region rather than a single pin on a map.

If you want to see movement fastest, the thinnest fields are the place to watch: Rome for aflac representative at one result, Cartersville at one to two results across three of the four terms, Dallas at three to five, and independent aflac agent nearly everywhere, where packs run 4 to 6 results deep instead of the usual 20.

Ask five clients for a review

On most of the reports in this chapter, reviews are a long grind against businesses with hundreds of them. On yours they are something closer to a shortcut, and the numbers are almost hard to believe.

Across all 302 top-five placements those four searches hand out, 300 show no review count at all. The highest total on any listing holding one of those spots, in any of the 16 towns, is four.

Five reviews would make your listing the most reviewed agent profile of its kind in this entire region on day one. You already have clients who would write them. This is the cheapest competitive advantage in the whole audit, and the window on it is open precisely because so few people in this category have gotten to it yet.
