What this means

The headline number is better than it first looks

You appear in 66 of 320 searches, which sounds modest until you look at where those appearances land. 50 of the 66 are top-three placements and 35 are outright first place. Most businesses in this audit appear far more often and convert far less. Almost every appearance you have is a strong one.

You hold top-three positions in 12 of the 16 markets measured: Rome, Lindale, Shannon, Cave Spring, Euharlee, Adairsville, Aragon, Cedartown, Summerville, Rockmart, Cartersville and Trion. For a school that has not yet opened its doors and has no reviews to its name, that is a real head start.

Cave Spring is already behaving like an established school

Your home market is the strongest by a wide margin: top three on 13 of 20 searches, first place on 8, and present in 15 of 20.

What makes Cave Spring important is not just the score, it is which searches are in it. You take first place there for elementary school, K-12 school, public school and free public school, alongside the charter terms. Those are ordinary parent searches, not insider vocabulary. Cave Spring is proof that a charter school can hold general school searches, and it is the template for what the surrounding towns could look like.

Four words are carrying almost everything

Forty of your 50 top-three placements come from four closely related searches:

  • public charter school, 12 top-three placements and 8 first places
  • new charter school, 11 and 9
  • charter school enrollment, 9 and 7
  • charter school, 8 and 7

These are excellent results and they should be protected. The limitation is that all four require the searching parent to already know the word charter and to already be looking for one. That is the informed end of the market. It is a good place to be strong and a difficult place to grow from, because the pool of people using those words is smaller than the pool of people choosing a school.

The general school searches are where the growth is, and where the wall is

The five grade-level searches, elementary school, middle school, high school, K-12 school and public school, cover 80 measured searches. You appear in 4 of them and hold the top three in 3, all three in Cave Spring.

Part of this is structural and should be understood plainly rather than treated as a target. In Rome, the search high school returns a single local result, Rome High School. The search middle school returns three, all district schools. These packs are largely filled by the assigned public schools a family is already zoned for, and that is how Google reads the category.

But Cave Spring shows the wall is not solid. You hold first place there for elementary school, K-12 school and public school. So it is achievable, and the difference between Cave Spring and everywhere else is worth understanding, because grade-level searches are what families use when they have not yet decided that a charter is what they want.

College prep was a deliberate test, and it came back as expected

College prep school was included on purpose to check whether those searches would reach a free public charter. They do not. You appear in 1 of 16 markets on that term and hold no top-three placement.

The reason is category rather than quality: those packs are built around tuition-charging private and parochial academies, which is a different kind of school offering a different kind of arrangement. This is not a gap in your visibility and it is not worth spending effort against. The same applies to best schools and school enrollment as bare phrases, which returned no appearances and, in one market, no local pack at all.

Reviews are not the currency in this category

Your profile currently carries no rating and no reviews, which is exactly what a school that has not opened should look like. It is worth knowing that this is not the disadvantage it would be in most industries.

Of the 399 distinct schools appearing across all these packs, 227 carry no rating at all. The majority of schools ranking in these searches, including the ones ranking above you, have no reviews either. Reputation signals are simply not how Google is sorting this category, so the absence of them is not what stands between you and better placement.

Distance is not the constraint either

In 62 of these searches, spread across 15 of the 16 markets, a school ranks in a town while sitting further from it than you do. Choice Educational Academy holds second place in Cedartown from 72.5 miles away, where you are 16.1 miles out. The Forest School: An Acton Academy ranks in Calhoun from 75.2 miles.

That matters for the four markets where you currently hold nothing. Calhoun, Cedar Bluff, Centre and Dallas are not out of reach because of geography, and in Calhoun in particular you are already close.

Where the map places you

Your profile has no public street address, so Google positions the listing from a map pin. That pin currently reads about 1.8 miles from downtown Rome and about 12.4 miles from Cave Spring. It reads 22.8 miles from both Calhoun and Cartersville.

This is worth a look, simply because the pin is what decides which towns Google considers you near. It is a small detail that quietly shapes every result above, and it is the kind of thing best settled early.

Three things that would move the needle

Speak the words parents use before they know the word charter

Your four charter terms produce 40 of your 50 top-three placements, which is excellent, but every one of them requires a parent who has already decided they want a charter school. The families you most need to reach, this first year and every year after it, are the ones still typing elementary school, middle school and public school.

Across those grade-level searches you appear in 4 of 80, and 3 of your top-three placements are all in Cave Spring, where you rank first for elementary school, K-12 school and public school. That is the proof it can be done. Building the plain grade-level language, the grades served and the free public school framing into how the profile and the site describe the academy is what lets those searches find you outside your home town. This is the difference between being findable by families already shopping for a charter and being findable by families simply shopping for a school.

Close the three Calhoun searches you nearly hold

Calhoun is the closest thing to a free market on the board. You hold no top-three placement there, but you are not far off: fourth for charter school, fourth for new charter school and fifth for charter school enrollment. Three searches, each within two positions of the visible block.

The schools ahead of you are Georgia-Cumberland Academy at 5.1 miles, Calhoun Falls Charter School, Mountain Education Charter High School and Mountain Education Chattooga Campus at 22.5 miles. That last one is the useful detail: it ranks in Calhoun from 22.5 miles away, and you are 22.8 miles away. You are not being outranked on distance there, you are being outranked on everything else. Calhoun is the largest market in this grid after Rome and Cartersville, and closing a two-position gap in a town where you already appear is a far shorter distance than opening new ground.

Put the map pin on the campus before the doors open

Your listing has no public street address, so the map pin is doing all the geographic work. It currently reads about 1.8 miles from downtown Rome and about 12.4 miles from Cave Spring, where the campus will be.

Because 62 of these searches are already being won by schools further away than you are, the pin and the stated service area are what Google is using to decide which towns you belong in. Confirming the pin sits on the campus, and that the profile states the communities the academy will draw from, is a short task with a large payoff, and it is worth settling before the first families arrive. It is also the one item on this list you can complete yourself this week.
