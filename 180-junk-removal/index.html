What this measures

When someone searches for a service near them, Google shows a short list of local businesses above the regular results. That short list is where the phone calls come from. Being fourth means being invisible.

Each search combined a service with a town — for example Dumpster Rental Rome GA or Junk Removal Cartersville GA. We recorded 180's position, and when 180 wasn't first, which company was. Positions come from Google Search itself, not from the Maps app, because Search is what most customers actually use.

Rome belongs to 180

In Rome, 180 is in the top 3 for 18 of 20 services and ranks first on 10 of them — including junk removal, hauling, pickup, furniture, appliances, garage cleanouts, yard waste, dumpster rental and shed removal. Five miles out in Lindale it holds 13 of 20. That is a dominant home market, built on a genuinely exceptional reputation.

The opportunity is that this strength stops almost exactly at the county line.

The reach problem isn't distance

180 doesn't reach the top 3 for a single service in 12 of the 15 towns. The natural assumption is that those towns are simply too far away. The searches show otherwise.

Competitors win these same towns from two to three times farther away than 180 is.

Several operators hold top spots in nine to eleven of these fifteen towns at once.

That means these markets are winnable from Rome. Google is willing to show a business across a wide area — 180 simply isn't set up to be shown there yet. The 482 five-star reviews aren't failing to travel; they're not being given the chance.

Dumpster searches are the softest spot at home

180 ranks first for junk removal in Rome, but on dumpster-specific searches — roll off, construction, residential, same day, 10 yard — it sits second or third behind smaller dumpster specialists. One of them ranks ahead on six of those searches with a fraction of the reviews.

Reviews aren't the deciding factor there, which is good news. These are high-intent commercial searches in the market 180 already owns, and the businesses ahead are winning them on something other than reputation — which means the gap is closable.

Two services worth stepping back from

Estate cleanouts and demolition return almost nothing outside Rome. Those searches are dominated by estate sale companies and by excavation contractors — different businesses answering a different need. Competing there means fighting for customers who aren't looking for a junk service.
