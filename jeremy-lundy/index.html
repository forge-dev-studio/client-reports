The short version

Your concern was that there may not be a market. There is one. It's just unclaimed.

People in your area search for supplemental insurance, benefits help, dental and vision coverage, and life insurance about 8,390 times every month. And 1,710 of those searches contain the word Aflac: people typing "aflac insurance," "aflac dental insurance," "aflac short term disability," or looking for an Aflac agent by name. These are buyers who already want your brand. That lane is standing empty: searches for an agent return no map results at all in six of your fourteen towns, and even "aflac insurance" itself returns unclaimed template listings with zero reviews everywhere it returns anything local.

We swept all of Georgia to see how the best agents in your field show up online. The most-reviewed agent listing we could find in the entire state holds four reviews. Not one has their own website. Meanwhile, in your towns, small independent brokers, including home-based ones with hidden addresses and as few as three reviews, are quietly holding top-three map spots for the broader supplemental searches.

The one-sentence summary: this is the emptiest competitive field we have measured in any industry, the searches are real, and the first agent who shows up properly wins it.
Where you stand today

You aren't losing these searches. You're not in them yet.

Across all 266 searches we measured, you appear zero times. Two things explain all of it:

Google has nothing of yours to show.

There is no business listing for you on Google today. Every search where a customer could find you, Google fills with someone else or with nothing. The good news is what our statewide sweep proved: the bar for owning this lane is lower than in any field we have measured.

Perspective

Three things the research settled.

1. Your field never showed up online. That is the opportunity.

Across eight Georgia metros, the best agent listing in your field holds four reviews, and none has a website. In six of your towns, the searches for an agent like you return no map results at all. Empty fields do not stay empty. The first agent who builds a real presence sets the bar everyone else has to clear.

2. A map audience and a web audience are two separate wins.

The brokers winning map spots in your towns have thin websites, and the firms with strong websites win business-buyer traffic through helpful content instead. The plan is built to take both: the map lane first, because it is nearly free to claim, and the content lane as your site grows.

3. AI answers are already deciding half of these searches.

46% of the searches we measured show a Google AI answer, 69% in Dallas itself, and on dental questions it passes 90%. Those answers quote websites that machines can read. A properly built site makes you quotable in the exact place these decisions now happen.
